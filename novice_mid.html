Mini Lesson: Interesting facts about Turkish Language and Culture

Turkish, as a language is a part of Turkic languages family which has 16 major dialects. Some other nations that speak a Turkic language dialect are Azerbaican, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, Tataristan etc.

The Turkish language is a mixture of Turkish, Arabic and Persian words. Before 1928 the Turkish alphabet was a modified version of the Arabic alphabet. After 1928 the alphabet was replaced with the current Latin based alphabet and the Language was reformed.

  • Turkey spans partially on Europe and Eurasia.
  • Istanbul is the only city in the world that spans on two continents.
  • Have you watched the movie "Troy"? Well, the real city Troy is in Turkey.
  • Santa Claus is a misspelling of Saint Nicolaus who was born in Patara in Turkey.
  • Mount Arararat where the Arc of Noah is believed to have crashed is in Turkey.
  • 70-letter Muvaffakiyetsizleştiricileştiriveremeyebileceklerimizdenmişsinizcesine, is a real word and can be transliterated as “as if you are from those we may not be able to easily make a maker of unsuccessful ones”

Sounds interesting? Let's dive in! I recommend that you start with vowels, consonants and such, but you are completely free to start from any mini lesson you like!
