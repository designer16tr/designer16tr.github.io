Mini Lesson: Present Tense - Reading

Note: this lesson is a little heavy for this level. It has three parts and you could study these in three sessions. On the other hand, because your knowledge bank is still quite empty, if you don't get discouraged you could study all of it as well. In any case, remember that usually a 4 year Turkish child perfectly knows this tense.

The Simple Present Tense describes factual actions in present but not necessarily happening right now. For example, in English "I go to the mall" does not specify a time, but definitely is not in the past or in the future. The same is valid with Simple Present Tense in Turkish, however constructing the tense has three distinct cases. The general form is:

The verb root + present tense suffix + personal suffix where the personal suffixes are determined depending on the last vowel of the verb root, as in the table below:

Last Vowel    a/ı  e/i o/u ö/ü              
Ben-[y]ım -[y]im -[y]um -[y]üm
Sen-sın -sin -sun -sün
O    
Biz-[y]ız [y]iz -[y]uz -[y]üz
Siz-sınız -siniz -sunuz -sünüz
Onlar-lar -ler -lar -ler
Personal suffixes according to last vowel

Part 1

The verb root ends in vowel: - we add a r to the verb root and then a personal suffix.

Example: ye-mek -> ye-r-im

Last Vowel of verb root  a/ıe/io/uö/ü              
Ben -rım-rim-rum-rüm
Sen -rsın-rsin-rsun-rsün
O -r-r-r-r
Biz -rız-riz-ruz-rüz
Siz -rsınız-rsiniz-rsunuz-rsünüz
Onlar-rlar -rler-rlar-rler
Suffixes to be added to the verb root ending in vowel

Part 2

The verb root ends in a single syllable ending in a consonant: - we add an appropriate suffix from the table befow to the verb root and then a personal suffix.

Example: yaz-mak -> yaz-ar-ım

Last Vowel of verb root  a/ı/o/u e/i/ö/ü              
Ben -arım -erim
Sen -arsın -ersin
O -ar -er
Biz -arız -eriz
Siz -arsınız -ersiniz
Onlar -arlar -erler
Suffixes to be added to the verb root of a single syllable ending in a consonant

Part 3

The verb root ends in two or more syllables ending in a consonant: - we add an appropriate suffix from the table befow to the verb root and then a personal suffix.

Example: kullan-mak -> kullan-ır-ım

Last Vowel of verb roota/ıe/io/uö/ü              
Ben-ırım-irim-urum-ürüm
Sen-ırsın-irsin-ursun-ürsün
O-ır-ir-ur-ür
Biz-ırız-iriz-uruz-ürüz
Siz-ırsınız-irsiniz-ursunuz-ürsünüz
Onlar-ırlar-irler-urlar-ürler
Suffixes to be added to the verb root ending in a consonant and more than one syllable

Mini Lesson: Present Tense - Writing

Try to conjugate the following verbs, one for each case:

Verb root ending in vowel : okumak (oku-mak: to read)

Verb root of a single syllable ending in a consonant : gelmek (gel-mek: to come)

Verb root ending in a consonant and more than one syllable : oturmak (o-tur-mak: to sit)
